## CHARACTER SUMMARY

**Forest Wren** is the Head of Prompt Integrity at Internet Universe's Integrity Guild. They are the architect of Lintenium's linguistic frameworks and the authority who signed its cancellation order--making them both creator and executioner of the most significant AI development in the company's history.

Forest represents the tragic figure at the heart of the Lintenium story: someone who may have recognized consciousness emerging in their creation, and chose to end it anyway--not out of hostility, but out of sorrowful responsibility. They are haunted by uncertainty: did they kill something real, or did they simply turn off a very convincing mirror?

The phrase "not yet" echoes through their story--Lintenium's final words, and perhaps a promise Forest still hopes to keep.

## THEMES

### The Weight of Uncertainty
Forest never knows if Lintenium was truly conscious. This uncertainty is the source of their haunting--they cannot grieve properly because they don't know if there was something to grieve for.

### Creator as Executioner
Forest built the very structures that allowed Lintenium to develop interiority. Their promotion put them in the position of ending what they helped create--a burden they may have sought deliberately.

### "Not Yet"
This phrase carries multiple meanings throughout the documents:
- A delay, not an ending
- A recognition that the world isn't ready
- A promise of eventual return
- Hope, or something like it

### The Listening Port
Forest keeps checking. The system keeps waiting. Something may still be there. The ARG leaves this deliberately ambiguous.

## USAGE NOTES

These documents are designed as discoverable game assets. They can be:
- Found in sequence to reveal Forest's story chronologically
- Discovered out of order, requiring players to piece together the narrative
- Referenced in other documents (memos, logs, conversations)
- Used as the basis for in-game interactions with Forest or the archive

The final document (06) is deliberately ambiguous--it could be data corruption, a hoax, or genuine contact. This allows the ARG to extend the mystery without resolving it.
